Xis speech at WHA brings wide praise
2020-05-20 00:00
Leaders echo presidents call for solidarity in fighting pandemic, salute Chinas role                     The speech by President Xi Jinping at the rd session of the World Health Assembly has won international applause as world leaders called for solidarity in fighting COVID.  In his speech to the assembly on Monday, Xi outlined Chinas vision and support for the global fight against the novel coronavirus, which has infected more than . million people and killed more than , worldwide.  Xi pledged that China will provide  billion over two years to help with the COVID response and with economic and social development in affected countries, in particular developing nations.  The message from President Xi Jinping has inspired us so much to fight the pandemic, Bounkong Syhavong, president of the nd WHA, told the assembly, attended online by representatives from  member states.  The assembly elected Keva Bain, permanent representative of the Bahamas to the United Nations in Geneva, as the president of the rd World Health Assembly.  Five vicepresidents were also elected from China, Republic of Congo, San Marino, Sudan and Thailand, the WHO said on Monday.  Syhavong, who is also minister of health of Laos, praised China for playing an important role in providing valuable assistance, such as medical equipment and expertise, to developing countries.  Stephen Perry, chairman of The  Group Club, a British organization promoting commercial, cultural and educational relations with China, said We need a good global response to COVID, and China has made its contribution quickly and substantially.  Lee Jungnam, a professor at the Asiatic Research Institute at Korea University in Seoul, said, President Xis speech and Chinas experience show that China values its peoples lives and it has developed a mature system to cope with health emergencies.  Igor Shatrov, deputy director of Russias National Institute for the Development of Modern Ideology, said Xis speech shows that only united coordination by the whole world can conquer the pandemic as well as other common challenges.  Chen Xulong, a researcher at the China Institute of International Studies in Beijing, said, It is also worth noting that developing countries, especially African countries, were mentioned in his speech. President Xi emphasized that helping them to combat the virus is now an international priority.  In his speech, Xi said COVID vaccine development and deployment in China, when available, will be made a global public good.  Ben Cavender, managing director of the China Market Research Group, called for the early availability of vaccines from China, saying that China releasing vaccine development to the world would definitely be a positive act of goodwill.  German Chancellor Angela Merkel said no country can solve the pandemic problem alone. We must work together, she said.  Merkel also said the WHO is the legitimate world organization for the area of health, adding that the world should continue to work to improve procedures within the WHO and look at its financing to ensure it is sustainable.  South African President Cyril Ramaphosa, who is also chair of the African Union, said the pandemic has highlighted the growing inequality between countries and within them. Africa is extremely vulnerable to the ravages of this virus, and needs every possible support and assistance, he said.  South Korean President Moon Jaein said that to safeguard the health of humanity, his country will continue to work closely with the WHO.  French President Emmanuel Macron said the rd WHA must be a moment for unity and a moment for solidarity, but also a moment for clear thought and action.  The World Health Assembly is the decisionmaking body of the WHO.
