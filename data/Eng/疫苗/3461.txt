Brazil approves vaccines amid surge in cases
2021-01-20 00:00
Nationwide inoculation program a welcome development due to increasing infection rate                    Brazilian authorities authorized the CoronaVac COVID vaccine made by Chinese pharmaceutical company Sinovac, as well as a second inoculation from Oxford University and AstraZeneca on Sunday. This was followed by the first vaccination to be administered in Brazil outside of a trial. Monica Calazans, a yearold nurse in Sao Paulo, was the first person to be given the Chinadeveloped jab minutes after Brazils federal health regulatory agency Anvisa gave the treatment the green light, along with the socalled Oxford vaccine. More than  Sao Paulo health workers went on to receive the CoronaVac shot after Calazans, whose vaccination occurred during a press event. Lets get vaccinateddont be afraid, Calazans said. A shipment of  million doses of the Oxford jab will soon arrive in the South American nation. Butantan Institute, a Sao Paulo based biological research center, has already begun manufacturing CoronaVac on Brazilian soil in partnership with Sinovac, according to media reports. The first wave of vaccinations is expected to involve around  million doses of CoronaVac. In a tweet, Sao Paulos governor Joao Doria applauded the development as a victory for science, a victory for Brazil, and a victory for life. The Oxford Vaccine Group wrote on Twitter that the team was very happy to see its vaccine gain approval and that it would eventually be distributed among all  states in Brazil. The commencement of a nationwide inoculation program is a welcome development for a country that has suffered high levels of infections and is battling the spread of a new COVID variant that is believed to be more transmissible. There were , new cases reported in Brazil on Sunday, bringing up the total to . million. Close to , people have died from the infection in Brazil, the secondhighest death toll behind the United States. Both the CoronaVac and Oxford vaccines are likely to play an important role in providing protection from the novel coronavirus for people in developing regions as they can be stored at normal refrigeration temperatures. This differs from the available treatments from Pfizer and Moderna, which require ultra cold storage and robust coldchain infrastructure for distribution. Both Indonesia and Turkey authorized CoronaVac this month. The Turkish health ministry said that more than , health workers have received the Sinovac vaccine since last week.
