New group to support UN peacekeepers
2021-04-29 00:00
China, three other nations help launch platform to ensure safety, security of troops                    A group to support United Nations peacekeepers was launched on Tuesday under the initiative of China, Brazil, Indonesia and Rwanda.The Group of Friends on the Safety and Security of United Nations Peacekeepers will provide a platform to strengthen information sharing, policy coordination and capacity building, said Ambassador Zhang Jun, Chinas permanent representative to the UN, at a virtual event on Tuesday.The Group of Friends comprises  countries and regions, including Russia, the United States, the United Kingdom, France, Germany, Japan, Morocco, South Africa, Pakistan, India, Egypt, Ethiopia and the League of Arab States.Keeping peacekeepers safe depends on the participation of all parties, Zhang said. The peacekeepers face threats in regional hot spots and from ethnic conflicts, terrorism and improvised explosive devices. The COVID pandemic has also heightened security risks, Zhang said.The UN peacekeepers were established in  to help countries torn by conflict to create conditions for lasting peace.More than  million people have served as UN peacekeepers, and more than , of them from over  countries have lost their lives in the cause of peace. This year,  peacekeepers have died, Zhang said.China is the secondlargest financial contributor to the peacekeeping budget and the largest troop contributor among the permanent members of the UN Security Council.Currently, more than , Chinese peacekeepers are serving on eight missions, performing duties such as patrolling, guarding, demining, medical treatment, engineering and air transport.Ambassador Zhang emphasized that the Group of Friends will study the new challenges in keeping peacekeepers safe, raise awareness and promote a common understanding among member states through the UN Security Council, the UN General Assembly and other platforms.Ensuring the safety and security of peacekeepers requires not only policy guidance but also practical action, Zhang said.He added that China is one of the champions of safety and security under the Action for Peacekeeping initiative, which has provided security and safety training for peacekeepers from many countries and funded the Secretariat in carrying out projects and studies.China also had decided to donate , doses of COVID vaccines to peacekeepers. Under Chinas initiative, the UN Security Council adopted the first resolution on peacekeeper security and safety.More than  guests attended the meeting chaired by Ambassador Zhang, including JeanPierre Lacroix, undersecretarygeneral for peace operations Gilles Michaud, undersecretarygeneral for safety and security Lisa Buttenheim, assistant secretarygeneral for support operations, as well as permanent representatives, deputy permanent representatives, military advisers and officials from members of the Group of Friends and other delegations.The participants spoke highly of Chinas leadership in establishing the group.
