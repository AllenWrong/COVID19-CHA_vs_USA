More steps taken to control borders
2020-03-18 00:00
The European Union was planning to shut its external borders as more countries and territories around the world imposed travel restrictions and closed borders to curb the spread of COVID.European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen proposed a day ban on incoming travel to the EU by foreigners, the Mirror reported.The less travel, the more we can contain the virus, von der Leyen said on Monday. Therefore... I propose to the heads of state and government to introduce temporary restriction on nonessential travel to the EU.The European Council was expected to vote on the proposal on Tuesday, CNN said.The proposal came as EU officials called for more coordination and insisted on the need to keep the nation blocs internal borders open as much as possible, as member states implement measures to slow the spread of the novel coronavirus.After the surge of COVID cases in Europe led the Italian government to put the country on lockdown, other member states have implemented strict measures and travel restrictions, including partially closing their borders.Figures released on Monday by the European Centre for Disease Prevention and Control show that , coronavirus cases have been reported in Europe, most of them in Italy, Spain, France and Germany, the Mirror reported. There have been , deaths, with the overwhelming majority in Italy.Speaking before a videoconference of the Group of Seven world leaders, Italian Prime Minister Giuseppe Conte warned that his country is entering the riskiest weeks of the outbreak.Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau, who remains in selfisolation, said on Monday the country will deny entry to all foreigners except Americans. The new measure will take effect on Wednesday, according to Bloomberg.In Asia, Malaysian Prime Minister Muhyiddin Yassin announced measures on Monday to restrict movement nationwide, from Wednesday until March , the Strait Times reported. All Malaysians will be barred from traveling abroad, and those returning from overseas must undergo a health inspection and selfquarantine for  days. There also will be a ban on all foreign tourists and visitors.Hong Kong Chief Executive Carrie Lam Cheng Yuetngor announced on Tuesday that all people entering the city starting at midnight on Thursday will be quarantined for  days, and she advised residents of the city to avoid all nonessential travel, Reuters reported.The new measures worldwide to seal off borders have left cruise ships stranded in the Caribbean, South America and Europe, with local governments refusing permission for passengers to disembark as more cases of infected passengers have come to light, according to the Associated Press.The Cruise Lines International Association said about  ships with , passengers were at sea as of last week.The United States, meanwhile, moved closer to a nationwide lockdown on Mondayeither governmentordered or voluntaryas fears over the outbreak led state officials to order businesses to close, keeping millions of Americans at home.Ohio Governor Mike DeWine said in a statement that the states primary polls were ordered not to open on Tuesday due to the outbreak, CNBC reported.DeWines announcement came after a judge rejected a lawsuit that sought to postpone the states primaries until June .Ohio was one of four states scheduled to hold presidential primaries on Tuesday. Arizona, Florida and Illinois were proceeding with their primary elections.US President Donald Trump said on Monday that the novel coronavirus outbreak in the US could last until July or August.US researchers opened the first tests of an experimental coronavirus vaccine with four healthy volunteers at the Kaiser Permanente Washington Research Institute in Seattle on Monday, AP said.Meanwhile, Japan has insisted that the Olympic Games will go ahead in Tokyo this summer, its confidence boosted by an early indication from the International Olympic Committeewhich met for emergency talks on Tuesdaythat ways may be explored to ensure safety at the event.After an unprecedented videoconference meeting with fellow Group of Seven leaders, Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe said on Tuesday that the leaders had agreed to support a complete Olympics, but avoided comment on the timing of the Games, which are scheduled for July.I want to hold the Olympics and Paralympics perfectly, as proof that the human race will conquer the virus, and I gained support for that from the G leaders, Abe told reporters after the conference.Julian Shea in London, Ai Heping in New York and Wang Xu in Tokyo
