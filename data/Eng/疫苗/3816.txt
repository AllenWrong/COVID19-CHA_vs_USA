Hong Kong to speed up vaccine registration
2020-12-14 00:00
Hong Kong will speed up the registration process of COVID vaccines in the city so that its residents will get vaccinated as soon as possible, Chief Secretary for Administration Matthew Cheung Kinchung wrote on his official blog on Sunday.Through a governmentled vaccination program, the majority of Hong Kong people can be vaccinated against the novel coronavirus by , Cheung said. The program will be voluntary and free for Hong Kong residents.The vaccine procurements will be able to give protection to the whole community with priority access to injections given to healthcare workers, senior citizens, eldercare center workers and people with chronic illnesses, he added.According to Cheung, the Hong Kong government had obtained an advance purchase agreement to get . million doses of the Sinovac Biotech Hong Kong vaccine, of which  million are expected to be delivered to Hong Kong in January  at the earliest.The vaccine will be produced in Daxing district, Beijing, according to a statement from the food and health authorities.Under an agreement with Fosun Pharma, another . million shots of the mRNA COVID vaccinejointly developed with German drug manufacturer BioNTech and produced in Europewill also be made available for Hong Kong. The first batch of  million doses of the vaccine is expected to be delivered as soon as the first quarter of next year.The government also secured a third deal of . million vaccine doses jointly developed by AstraZeneca and the University of Oxford. The vaccines will be delivered to Hong Kong in batches by the end of the second quarter of next year at the earliest.Hong Kong Chief Executive Carrie Lam Cheng Yuetngor wrote on her social media account on Saturday that all vaccines must be evaluated by experts in a scientific manner based on data, to confirm that they are safe for use, regardless of the research company, the technology applied, or the manufacturing location.Lam said she and her administration team will receive the vaccination once the shots are approved for emergency use in Hong Kong, as a way to encourage more residents to join the vaccination program.Local resident Kuk Tinman, who learned of the news with family members, said they were quite excited to hear about the vaccine procurements. Kuk, in her s, said she would apply to receive the first batch of vaccines.She also expressed confidence in the quality of vaccines produced by Sinovac, citing the Chinese mainlands success in controlling the pandemic.Pang Kongcheung, a yearold Hong Kong resident, said the arrival of the vaccines will help lift spirits in Hong Kong amid the fourth wave of the outbreak in the city.Pang, who returned to Hong Kong in March from Hubei province with help from the government, said he believes the vaccines will give local residents, especially seniors, more protection and help ease public anxiety.Hong Kong recorded  new COVID infections on Sunday, bringing the citys tally of confirmed cases to ,.Li Bingcun contributed to this story.Contact the writers at gumengyanchinadailyhk.com                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          !CDATA            function shareweixin              var ua  navigator.userAgent.toLowerCase              ifiphoneipadipod.testua  ua.matchMicroMessengeri  micromessenger                window.opena.app.qq.comosimple.jsp?pkgnamecom.theotino.chinadailyiosschemeencodeIos                            else ifandroid.testua  ua.matchMicroMessengeri  micromessenger                window.opena.app.qq.comosimple.jsp?pkgnamecom.theotino.chinadailyandroidschemeencodeAndroid                            else                window.openwww.chinadaily.com.cnmobiledaily.html
