Mainland willing to aid Taiwan compatriots on COVID spokesperson
2021-05-18 09:30
Lee Chenghung, the president of the Association of Taiwan Investment Enterprises on the Mainland ATIEM, receives a dose of COVID vaccine at a community health center in Malu town of Jiading new city, East Chinas Shanghai, April , . PhotoXinhua    BEIJING  A Chinese mainland spokesperson on Monday expressed the mainlands willingness to provide assistance to Taiwan compatriots in quickly defeating the COVID epidemic. Zhu Fenglian, a spokesperson for the Taiwan Affairs Office of the State Council, was responding to a media query on a recent appeal from Taiwans New Party and other social groups to the Democratic Progressive Party authority regarding the receipt of COVID vaccines from the mainland. Expressing the mainlands concern over Taiwans recent severe epidemic situation, Zhu said she hoped Taiwan compatriots would be able to contain the virus as soon as possible and return to normal life. It is urgent to remove political obstacles on the island so that Taiwan compatriots can gain access to the vaccine and their health and wellbeing can be guaranteed, Zhu said.
