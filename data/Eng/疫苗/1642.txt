Mainland ready to do best to help Taiwan on COVID vaccination Spokesperson
2021-06-11 15:13
Photo taken on June ,  shows a vial of the Sinopharm vaccine in Beijing, capital of China. PhotoXinhua    BEIJING  A Chinese mainland spokesperson on Friday expressed the mainlands willingness to do its best to help Taiwan compatriots on COVID vaccination. Ma Xiaoguang, a spokesperson for the Taiwan Affairs Office of the State Council, said Taiwan compatriots who fly to the mainland on civilian passenger flights can get inoculated here on voluntary basis, as long as they meet vaccination requirements and abide by epidemic control rules for inbound travels. Around , Taiwan compatriots had been vaccinated on the mainland as of May , said Ma, responding to a media question on Taiwans acute vaccine shortage. Noting many people on the island had expressed their willingness to see early shipments of mainland vaccines, he urged the Democratic Progressive Party authority in Taiwan to remove obstacles as soon as possible.
