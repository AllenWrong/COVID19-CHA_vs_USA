White House official says US not going to control pandemic
2020-10-27 15:50
White House Chief of Staff Mark Meadows attends US President Donald Trumps campaign event, in Lititz, Pennsylvania, US, Oct , . PhotoAgencies    WASHINGTON  White House Chief of Staff Mark Meadows said Sunday that the United States is not going to control the spread of COVID at home, as infections continue to surge in the country. We are not going to control the pandemic. We are going to control the fact that we get vaccines, therapeutics and other mitigation areas, Meadows said in an interview on CNNs State of the Union. It is a contagious virus just like the flu, he said, adding that What we need to do is make sure that we have the proper mitigation factors, whether its therapies or vaccines or treatments to make sure that people dont die from this.
