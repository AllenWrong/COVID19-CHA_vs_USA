China pledges to pull its weight in COVID fight
2020-09-27 01:47
Chinese medical team members discuss COVID testing and treatment with Palestinian health officials in the West Bank city of Ramallah in June, . PhotoXinhua    China will fully implement the measures announced by President Xi Jinping on international cooperation on fighting COVID, State Councilor and Foreign Minister Wang Yi said on Saturday. Wang made the remarks at the Highlevel Video Conference on Poverty Eradication and SouthSouth Cooperation. In the face of the outbreak of the pandemic, China has launched the largestever emergency humanitarian campaign since the founding of the Peoples Republic of China in , Wang said. China has dispatched  medical teams to  countries, and it has offered  batches of antipandemic aid to  countries and four international organizations, he said. China will fully implement the measures announced by President Xi at the th General Assembly of the World Health Organization and at the general debate of the th United Nations General Assembly, Wang said. China will provide  billion of international aid this year, build  cooperation links between China and African countries, and make efforts to set up a global humanitarian response depot and hub in China, he said. Several COVID vaccines developed by China are in Phase III clinical trials, and when they are available for use, these vaccines will be made a global public good, and they will be provided to other developing countries on a priority basis, Wang said. China will provide another  million to the UN COVID Global Humanitarian Response Plan, providing  million to the ChinaFAO SouthSouth Cooperation Trust Fund Phase III and extending the Peace and Development Trust Fund between the UN and China by five years after it expires in , he said, adding that all of these measures announced by Xi will be implemented by China without any discount.
