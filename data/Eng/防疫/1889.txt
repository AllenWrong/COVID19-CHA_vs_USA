EU raises COVID risk level, insists not to close interior borders
2020-03-03 09:05
People wear masks as they walk in Milan, Italy, February , . PhotoXinhua    BRUSSELS, March  Xinhua  The risk for people in Europe to be infected with the COVID was leveled up by the European Center for Disease Prevention Control ECDC on Monday, but the European Union EU insisted not to close the borders between its member states. According to the daily ECDC risk assessment, the risk associated with COVID infection for people in the EUEEAEuropean Economic Area and UK is currently considered moderate to high. In its previous assessment published on Feb , the assessment was low to moderate. In other words the virus continues to spread, European Commission President Ursula von der Leyen told a press conference in Brussels.
