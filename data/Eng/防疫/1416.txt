Intelligent technology used in media center
2021-11-30 00:00
The Main Media Center for the forthcoming  Beijing Winter Olympic Games is set to provide journalists and photographers an experience like no other as they broadcast their stories to an estimated global audience of more than  billion people. The media center, which covers an area of , square meters, will be the temporary home and office of some , journalists from more than  countries and regions, and , broadcasters from over  networks. What makes this media center different from those in the past is its reliance on and utilization of intelligent technologies across a wide array of areas. Armies of robots will be used to provide everything from directions and COVID prevention and control, to food delivery and even food preparation. During the Games, a pandemic prevention tracking system will be used along with an entrance and exit control system on site, to ensure epidemic prevention and to track and trace any issues should they arise. The canteen for the media workers is also completely automated, with robots that are capable of preparing meals from hamburgers to clay pot rice via a simple scan of a QR code. The ,sqm canteen can serve thousands of people at a time. The media center features a comprehensive and detailed data system, which allows for the unified monitoring, scheduling and realtime checking of the status of all the Olympic venues. According to Zhang Lidong, director of the engineering department at Beijing North Star Events Investment, the owner of the media center, the facilities have all been tested and are ready to go. More than  Olympic broadcast service team members are already working inside the media center, Zhang said.
