Mask cant hide identity on Harbin subway
2020/7/2 18:37
Facial recognition clears a passenger for the subway in Harbin, Heilongjiang province, on Wednesday. Photo provided to chinadaily.com.cn Starting Wednesday, passengers in Harbin, Heilongjiang province were able to use facial recognition to board a subway and pay the fare, even without removing their masks, Harbin Metro Group reported. The technology has been installed at all  stations of Harbins Line  and Line  subways, making the city the first in China to introduce facial recognition technology that works even for people who wear masks. After downloading the application  called Zhihuixing  to a mobile phone and registering personal information and completing the online registration with facial recognition authentication, passengers can experience new convenience and speed in entering and exiting stations. The technology can help passengers avoid direct contact with tickets, bank notes and mobile phones, which can carry coronavirus, and will effectively reduce the risk of crossinfection during the epidemic.
