Japan lifts COVID state of emergency for Osaka, Kyoto, Hyogo prefectures
2020/5/21 18:20
A couple, wearing protective masks following an outbreak of the coronavirus disease COVID, smile at Kiyomizudera Buddhist temple in Kyoto, Japan, March , .  TOKYO  The Japanese government on Thursday lifted a state of emergency in Osaka, Kyoto and Hyogo prefectures in western Japan as the number of COVID infections there has remained low. Japanese Prime Minister Shinzo Abe told reporters that the emergency measure may end in Tokyo and its three prefectural neighbors Chiba, Saitama, and Kanagawa as well as the northernmost prefecture of Hokkaido as early as Monday after a fresh review by a government panel of health experts. Abe first declared a monthlong state of emergency for Tokyo and six other prefectures on April , but this was expanded to cover the entire nation on April . The state of emergency was originally scheduled to end on May , and the government extended it through May . Last Thursday, the Japanese government decided to lift the state of emergency ahead of the planned deadline for  of the countrys  prefectures as the coronavirus has been less prevalent in those areas.
