Putin, Trump, King Salman agree to coordinate on oil
2020/4/10 7:48
A worker wears a protective mask, following an outbreak of coronavirus, at Nahr Bin Umar oil field, north of Basra, Iraq March , .  MOSCOW  Russia, the United States and Saudi Arabia have agreed to coordinate actions on stabilizing oil markets and minimize the impact of oil price volatility on global economy, Russian presidents press office said in a statement on Thursday. Today, Russian President Vladimir Putin had a phone conversation with US President Donald Trump and Saudi Arabia King Salman bin Abdulaziz Al Saud, the statement said. Oil prices have plummeted amid the COVID outbreak as global fuel demand has plunged due to air traffic cut and production shutdown.
