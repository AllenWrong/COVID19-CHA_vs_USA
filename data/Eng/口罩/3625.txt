A man checks his body temperature to enter to the Prado museum as it reopens to the public under strict social distance measures, amid the coronavirus disease COVID outbreak, in Madrid, Spain, June , .  MADRID  People who do not wear a face mask in closed public areas will face fines of up to  euros , according to the Spanish governments draft plans appearing in several Spanish newspapers on Saturday. Prime Minister Pedro Sanchez confirmed during the week that his cabinet would approve the measures to be put into effect in Spain when the State of Alarm and the special lockdown measures it contains expires on June . Among the new normality measures are fines for those who dont wear face masks in closed public areas and outdoors where it is impossible to maintain a safe distance of two meters. In workplaces, employers will be obliged to provide adequate hygiene and cleanliness and ensure that workers are able to maintain social distancing and have access to soap, water, and hand gels. Employers will be required to take measures to avoid overcrowding. Similar measures are to be put into place for schools and educational centers. Commercial centers will have to pay special attention to food markets and common areas, and limit the number of people allowed into a center at any one time. Travel between Spains  Autonomous Communities will be allowed once all of them have passed the four stages in Spains plan to relax restrictions. However, passenger numbers will still be limited on long and medium distance trains and also on busses carrying out intercity routes. Meanwhile, airlines will be obliged to keep passenger lists for a month after a flight, for the sake of tracking those who have contact with an infected passenger.
